Olanzapine MD vs. SOT: Impact on BMI in patients with psychiatric illness
by Medeka | November 01, 2013
PLATYPUS study
A 16-week, double-dummy, randomized trial assessed the change in body mass index (BMI) in patients taking SOT with those patients who shifted to olanzapine MD from SOT. Patients (n=149) included in the study had either schizophrenia or bipolar disorder and had been taking 5–20 mg SOT daily for ≥4 weeks and ≤52 weeks. Further, patients had a weight gain of ≥5 kg and a change of ≥1 kg/m2 BMI at baseline. All 149 patients were randomized to receive either olanzapine MD plus placebo (n=84) or sublingual placebo plus SOT (n=65). Mean weight, BMI, and waist circumference of the study population at baseline were 81.1 kg, 28.1 kg/m2, and 96.6 cm respectively. Similarly, mean daily olanzapine MD and SOT doses prescribed were 13.87 and 13.23 mg respectively.4

  • Better compliance and QOL with olanzapine MD than SOT
    • Compliance rate was higher for Olanzapine MD than SOT therapy (92.9 vs. 78.5%; p=0.015).4
    • Clinical efficacy (measured by Clinical Global Impression-Severity [CGI-S] scale and Global Assessment of Functioning [GAF] scale scores was found to be similar in both groups. Patients in both groups experienced improvements in their symptoms and quality of life (QOL; Table 1). 4
