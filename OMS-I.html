Olanzapine MD vs. SOT: Impact on BMI in patients with psychiatric illness
by Medeka Health | November 01, 2013
Introduction
Weight gain is commonly seen in patients using atypical antipsychotics.1 Risk of weight gain is higher with olanzapine and clozapine.2 Despite its potential for weight gain, olanzapine is used worldwide due to its favorable benefit/risk profile. Among the different formulations of olanzapine, the oral formulations include standard olanzapine coated tablets (SOT), olanzapine mouth dissolving tablet (MD), and granules.3 Several evidences suggest that olanzapine MD and SOT show a difference in treatment-emergent changes in weight gain.4 But a few studies state that patients switching from SOT to olanzapine MD experience lesser weight gain or loss.4,5 Researchers hypothesized that partial bypassing of gastrointestinal metabolism of olanzapine MD may be responsible for the decrease in the risk of weight gain. Partial bypassing of olanzapine MD results in differential gastric absorption reducing the chance of olanzapine reaching putative pyloric 5-HT2-like receptors, which are otherwise involved in weight gain. In this milieu, a landmark clinical trial, Placebo and Active Comparator Trial of Olanzapine Zydis Pills Used Sublingually (PLATYPUS) reiterates the evidences on weight gain with SOT and in patients switching from SOT to olanzapine MD.4
