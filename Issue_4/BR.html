by Medeka Health | November 01, 2013
The Bipolar Brain
Integrating Neuroimaging and Genetics

The Bipolar Brain is an excellent up-to-date compendium that provides substantial advances in understanding the brain structures, neuroimaging, and genetics involved in the clinical manifestations of bipolar disorder. The book also provides an integrated neurophysiological paradigm of bipolar disorder by focusing on neuroimaging and genetic findings. The neurophysiological model suggested in the book can be a valuable resource for clinicians to a new framework for understanding bipolar illness and to develop improved therapies or novel strategies to treat/prevent the bipolar disorder. The content of the book is divided into three major

  • How neuroimaging informs models of bipolar disorder: Discusses brain imaging techniques and their application in bipolar disorder, structural brain abnormalities, advances in functional neuroimaging, including neuroimaging studies of bipolar disorder in youth, in bipolar and unipolar depression, and in bipolar disorder and schizophrenia.
  • How genetic research informs models of bipolar disorder: Discusses fundamental genetics of bipolar disorder, genetic techniques and their application to bipolar disorder and association of mitochondrial genetics and bipolar disorder.
  • Integration and future directions: Discusses the roles of integrating imaging techniques and genetic research towards personalized medicine and suggest an integrated neurophysiological model of bipolar disorder.
    •

The book is highly recommended for physicians who manage patients with bipolar disorder.
