by Medeka Health | February 01, 2014
Managing MDDassociated insomnia: Role of zolpidem

In patients with insomnia and mild-to-moderate MDD, insomnia should not be treated as a symptom of MDD, but each condition should be treated independently.1 Concomitant treatment of a nonbenzodiazepine hypnotic (such as zolpidem) and an antidepressant (such as escitalopram) can be a valuable treatment option in such patients.1 Various studies have demonstrated that zolpidem extended-release (ER) is safe and effective in the treatment of patients with insomnia:

  • A double-blind, placebo-controlled, parallelgroup study (n=192) showed that zolpidem improves sleep maintenance, sleep induction, and duration of sleep in patients with primary insomnia.4
  • A phase II, double-blind, randomized, placebo-controlled study reported that zolpidem ER 12.5 mg 3 to 7 nights per week for up to 6 months provided sustained and significant improvements in onset and maintenance of sleep and also improved nextday concentration and morning sleepiness.5
  • Encouraging results were observed with escitalopram/zolpidem ER concomitant therapy in patients with insomnia and generalized anxiety disorder. Patients showed significantly improvement in insomnia and sleep-related next-day symptoms.6
