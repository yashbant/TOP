Management of insomnia in depressed patients and its effect on next-day functioning
by Medeka Health | February 01, 2014
Concomitant therapy with zolpidem ER and escitalopram improves insomnia and sleeprelated next-day functioning in patients with MDD

Fava et al conducted a 24-week, 2-phase, doubleblind, randomized, placebo-controlled study to determine the efficacy and safety of zolpidem in reducing the symptoms of insomnia in patients with MDD-associated insomnia.6 Enrolled patients (n=385; age: 21–64 years) received open-label escitalopram 10 mg/d and were randomized to receive adjunctive double-blind treatment with zolpidem extended-release 12.5 mg/night (n=193) or placebo (n=192) for 8 weeks (phase 1). Patients with ≥ 50% decrease from baseline in 17-item Hamilton Depression Rating Scale (HDRS17 score) were considered as responders. Patients responding to the assigned treatment in phase 1 were continued for 16 weeks of double-blind treatment (phase 2; zolpidem n=96; placebo n=95). In each treatment phase, patients were evaluated for change from baseline in:

  • Total sleep time
  • Sleep-onset latency
  • Number of awakenings
  • Wake time after sleep onset
  • Sleep quality
  • Sleep-related next-day functioning
  • HDRS17 score
  • Sleep Impact Scale (SIS) score
  • Patient and Clinical Global Impressions of Insomnia Treatment (PGI-IT scale)
  • Clinician’s ratings of severity of mental illness and global improvement of mental illness measured by Clinical Global Impression (CGI) scales
  • Massachusetts General Hospital Cognitive and Physical Functioning Questionnaire (MGHCPFQ) score
  • Quality of Life Enjoyment and Satisfaction Questionnaire (Q-LES-Q) score
