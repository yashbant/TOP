Management of insomnia in depressed patients and its effect on next-day functioning
by Medeka Health | February 01, 2014
Concomitant therapy with zolpidem ER and escitalopram improves insomnia and sleeprelated next-day functioning in patients with MDD
Effect on symptoms of depression

Depression relapse rates between the groups were almost twice as many relapses in the placebo/ escitalopram group, suggesting that this difference might have become significant with a longer followup period.6

  • Decreases from baseline HDRS17 total scores were comparable between zolpidem ER/ escitalopram and placebo/escitalopram groups.
  • By the end of week 1, higher number of patients in the zolpidem group met the response criteria for depression treatment (≥ 50% decrease in HDRS17 total scores) than the placebo/escitalopram group (responder rate: 63.7 vs 58.4%).
  • The rates of remission (HDRS17 total score ≤ 7) at the end of phase 1 were 45 and 54.5% in patients treated with placebo/escitalopram and zolpidem ER/escitalopram respectively.
  • The rates of depression relapse after 24 weeks of treatment were 6.4% in the placebo/ escitalopram group and 3.2% in zolpidem ER/escitalopram group.
  • PGI-IT data revealed that patients in the zolpidem ER/escitalopram group rated their insomnia treatment significantly superior than the placebo/escitalopram group (p < 0.0001 during phase 1; p < 0.001 during phase 2), in concordance with their improved sleep characteristics (Figure 2)

    .
  • Clinician’s ratings of severity of mental illness (CGI-S) and global improvement of mental illness (CGI-I) were comparable between the two groups throughout the study period.
Safety and tolerability

The safety and tolerability of the combination therapy of zolpidem ER and escitalopram were generally comparable to those with placebo and escitalopram. The most common adverse events associated with combination treatment of zolpidem ER and escitalopram included nausea, somnolence, dry mouth, dizziness, fatigue, and amnesia. Study discontinuations due to treatment related adverse events were non-significantly higher among patients in the zolpidem ER group than in the placebo group.6
