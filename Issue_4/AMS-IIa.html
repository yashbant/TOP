Meta-analysis comparing the efficacy and tolerability of 15 antipsychotic drugs

Acceptability of amisulpride is best compared to other drugs3

All-cause discontinuation which encompasses efficacy and tolerability was used to determine the acceptability of treatments.

  • All drugs, except zotepine, were significantly better than placebo for allcause discontinuation (Figure 2)

  • Amisulpride had the lowest risk of allcause discontinuation when compared to the other drugs (odds ratio [OR]: 0.33; number needed to treat [NNT]: 6; (Figure 2)

  • Haloperidol had the highest risk of allcause discontinuation (OR: 0.80; NNT: 20; (Figure 2).


Amisulpride has the lowest risk for sedation3

Sedation is an unpleasant adverse effect of antipsychotic drugs.

  • Clozapine (OR: 8.82), zotepine (OR: 8.15), and chlorpromazine (OR: 7.56) showed a higher tendency to cause sedation (Figure 3)

  • Amisulpride (OR: 1.42, Figure 3

    ) showed the lowest tendency to cause sedation, which may be attributed to the absence of H1 blockade (ie, histaminergic receptors associated with sedation)
  • Sedative effect of amisulpride was not significantly more than that of placebo.
