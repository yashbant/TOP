Options for switching antipsychotic drugs in schizophrenia
by Medeka Health | February 01, 2014
Summary

Switching from one antipsychotic to another is done to improve or maintain symptomatic and functional level of a patient while improving tolerability. Switching is indicated in patients with persistent symptoms (positive and negative), side effects, relapse, affective syndromes, organic brain syndrome, and in young patients. Amisulpride is a superior switch option with high efficacy against negative symptoms and very few extrapyramidal side effects. Amisulpride is a good first-line treatment choice because of its rapid onset of action; and its effects on positive, negative, affective and cognitive symptoms; and its tolerability.12 Amisulpride has a lower risk of drug interactions with other antipsychotics and is associated with lesser hospital admission rates after the switch. Risperidone, in the hierarchy of atypical antipsychotics, causes the highest prolactin elevation. Amisulpride has similar efficacy as risperidone, but causes lesser weight gain and also lowers heart rate and blood pressure.
