Normalization of reward related activities in schizophrenia - Clinical improvements with Amisulpiride
by Medeka Health | February 01, 2014
Role of amisulpride in improving reward-related brain activations
Findings of fMRI
  • Salience contrast showed differences between groups at baseline.
  • At baseline, activation in the salience contrast was lesser among patients as compared to healthy controls.
  • At follow-up, no differences were reported in salience contrast among groups.
  • Maximum, mean, or cumulative head movements (repeated-measures) were similar in patients and controls.
Interaction in the vs region of interest

The effect of antipsychotic treatment on D2 blockade in the right vs region was of primary interest in the study. An analysis of the vs region revealed a significant group time interaction in the salience contrast (p=0.04, Figure 1).

  • Patients displayed an increase in the salience contrast activation over time
  • Healthy controls displayed a decrease in the salience contrast activation over time
Association to psychopathology
  • A significant correlation was observed between the improvement of positive symptoms and change anticipation-related fMRI signal in the right vs region Figure 2).

    .
  • Patients with the greatest improvements in positive symptoms had the highest increase in reward-related fMRI signal in the right vs region.
