Options for switching antipsychotic drugs in schizophrenia
by Medeka Health | February 01, 2014
Switching to amisulpride

Amisulpride has a unique selective pharmacological profile and effectively treats positive, negative, as well as depressive symptoms. It has high efficacy against negative symptoms and also causes very few extrapyramidal side effects.5 It is also a favourable switch option when cardiovascular and anticholinergic side-effects are the treatment limiting factors.3,5 Amisulpride is a superior switch option in cases where the response is not satisfactory with other antipsychotics.3,5 In a study evaluating switching to amisulpride in patients with clozapine resistant schizophrenia by comparing scores before switching (baseline) and scores 8 weeks post switching (end point) showed statistically significant improvement in the mean scores (Figure 2).6

When switching to amisulpride, a cross tapering approach is preferred. Due to low plasma protein binding and poor metabolism, amisulpride has a decreased risk of drug interactions. Hence, it can be initiated using a 4-week overlap with the earlier antipsychotic.7 The initiation dose of amisulpride is 400–800 mg for positive symptoms and 50–300 mg for negative symptoms. Post the switch over, amisulpride has been shown to reduce the number of hospital admissions. Additionally, when compared to switching to other antipsychotics, switching to amisulpride reduces the risk of drug interactions and withdrawal symptoms.3
