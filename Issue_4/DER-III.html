Evidence for first-line treatment option for rapid cycling bipolar disorder
CANMAT guidelines recommend divalproex as the first-line therapy for rapid cycling bipolar disorder
by Medeka Health | February 01, 2014
Lithium vs divalproex for long-term maintenance

A 20-month, double-blind, parallel-group study compared the efficacy of divalproex and lithium in the management of patients with recently stabilized rapid-cycling bipolar disorder. In the study, 60 patients were stratified based on illness type (bipolar I vs bipolar II disorder) and were randomized to receive either lithium or divalproex monotherapy in a balanced design. Findings revealed that:7

  • Overall, 53% of patients reported a relapse into any mood; 59% of patients displayed a depressive relapse, while 41% displayed hypomanic/manic/mixed relapse
  • The rates of relapse into any mood episode were 56 and 50% for patients treated with lithium and divalproex respectively (Figure 2).

  • The rate of depressive relapse was 34% for patients treated with lithium and 29% for patients treated with divalproex (Figure 2).

  • The rate of hypomania/mania relapse was 19% for patients treated with lithium and 22% for patients treated divalproex (Figure 2).

  • The rate of treatment discontinuation due to side effects was 16% for lithium and 4% for divalproex (Figure 2).
