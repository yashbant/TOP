Management of insomnia in depressed patients and its effect on next-day functioning
by Medeka Health | February 01, 2014
Concomitant therapy with zolpidem ER and escitalopram improves insomnia and sleeprelated next-day functioning in patients with MDD
Effect on symptoms of insomnia

Treatment with zolpidem ER/escitalopram offered significantly greater improvement in total sleep than the treatment with placebo/escitalopram. The mean change from baseline total sleep time during phase 1 was 37.9–45.5 minutes more in zolpidem ER/escitalopram as compared to zolpidem ER/placebo group (p < 0.0001). The superiority of zolpidem ER/escitalopram in total sleep time was maintained through week 16 (p < 0.05). At the end of phase 2, the mean difference between the zolpidem ER/escitalopram group and the placebo/escitalopram group was 20 minutes (Figure 1).6

Other sleep characteristics including sleep onset latency, wake time after sleep onset, number of awakenings, and sleep quality were significantly improved in patients treated with zolpidem ER/ escitalopram (p < 0.001 for all) during phase 1. Further, patients in the zolpidem ER/escitalopram treatment group continued to exhibit greater improvement in number of awakenings and sleep quality throughout phase 2 (p ≤ 0.001). Patients treated with zolpidem ER/escitalopram also showed significant improvement in insomnia-only HDRS17 items throughout phase 1 (p < 0.001) and phase 2 (p < 0.05).6

Effect on next-day functioning

Besides complaints of insomnia, patients with MDD also suffer from symptoms of impaired functioning, including diminished ability to think or concentrate. Fava et al demonstrated that treatment with zolpidem ER/escitalopram had a positive impact on several sleep-related next-day functioning measures.6

  • Significantly greater improvements were reported during phase 1 and phase 2 in all the SIS domain scores in patients treated with zolpidem ER/escitalopram as compared to placebo/escitalopram ([p < 0.5] except mental fatigue).
  • Zolpidem ER/escitalopram therapy was associated with significantly greater improvements in the MGH-CPFQ total score, wakefulness/alertness, energy, memory/recall, and mental acuity at week-8 as compared to placebo/escitalopram (p < 0.05).
  • Zolpidem ER/escitalopram treatment resulted in significantly greater improvements in several aspects of sleep-related next-day functioning, specifically morning energy (p < 0.05), impact of insomnia on daily activities (p < 0.05), and morning concentration ability than the placebo/escitalopram treatment.
  • The degree of improvement in functioning and quality of life on the Q-LES-Q assessments was similar in zolpidem ER and placebo groups.
