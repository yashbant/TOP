Montgomery-Asberg Depression Scale (MADRS)
by Medeka Health | February 01, 2014

The clinician-rated Montgomery-Asberg Depression Rating Scale (MADRS) has items on the scale progress from broadly-phrased questions about symptoms to more detailed ones which allow a precise rating of severity. The rater must decide whether the rating lies on the defined scale steps (0, 2, 4, 6) or between them (1, 3, 5). Approximately 15 minutes are required to complete rating the scale. A score greater than 30 or 35 on the MADRS indicates severe depression, while a score of 10 or below indicates remission. MADRS is as sensitive as Hamilton Depression rating scale for detecting antidepressant efficacy
