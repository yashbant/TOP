Safety of an antidepressant in CVD and stroke patients: Outcomes from DECARD study
by Medeka Health | February 01, 2014
Depression in patients with stroke

Depression is reported by more than 50% of all patients who have experienced a stroke. Numerous studies have shown that poststroke depression is associated with impaired recovery in activities of daily living and increased mortality. Thus, prevention of depression is potentially an important goal. Escitalopram has been shown to prevent the onset of depressive disorders in patients with an index stroke.4

Safety of escitalopram in poststroke patients with depression

Jorge et al conducted a 12-month randomized study to determine the effect of escitalopram on cognitive outcome in 129 patients who were treated within 3 months following stroke. Patients were assigned to either a double-blind treatment with escitalopram (n=43) or placebo (n=45), or a nonblinded arm of problem-solving therapy (n=41). At the end of treatment, change in RBANS (Repeatable Battery for the Assessment of Neuropsychological Status) total score and RBANS delayed memory score showed no differences between the 3 groups (p < 0.01). The researchers reported that escitalopram had a significant effect on the change in RBANS total score and the change in RBANS delayed memory score. Researchers reported no differences between groups in the frequency of any of the events, especially cardiovascular adverse effects in poststroke patients (Table 1).

Treatment with escitalopram showed improvement in verbal and visual memory functions (global cognitive functioning), which was independent of its effect on depression in stroke patients.2
