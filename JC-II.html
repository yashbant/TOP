Lactate levels are elevated in patients with bipolar disorder
Increased lactate levels in brain tissue and cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) is a biomarker of metabolic dysfunction. Chu et al. investigated the distribution of lactate in bipolar (n=21) and healthy brains (n=10) using 2D-proton magnetic resonance spectroscopy imaging (1H MRSI), a multi-voxel magnetic resonance spectroscopy (MRS) technique.

Out of 21 patients with bipolar disorder, imaging reports of 9 patients showed lactate signals in 21 voxels. Of the 9 patients, 7 of them were euthymic and 2 had a mixed episode. No significant difference was noted between affective and euthymic patients (p=0.68) or medicated and unmedicated patients (p=1.0). In healthy controls, only 3 of the 10 people showed lactate signals. Lactate signals in bipolar patients were observed mostly in the caudate and anterior cingulate cortex (ACC) regions.

Bipolar patients had higher Lac/N-acetyl aspartate [NAA] ratio than healthy controls (0.19±0.09 vs. 0.12±0.01; p=0.04). Similarly, Lac/ phosphocreatine + creatine [PCr+Cr] ratio was higher for bipolar patients than healthy controls (0.35±0.14 vs. 0.22±0.04; p=0.04). On examining only the caudate, bipolar patients had higher Lac/ NAA and Lac/PCr+Cr ratios than healthy controls (Lac/NAA: 0.20±0.07 vs. 0.11±0.01, p=0.06; Lac/ [PCr+Cr]: 0.38±0.13 vs. 0.22±0.04, p=0.06).

Hence, the study findings suggested that lactate signals localized in the frontal-subcortical circuit may be responsible for metabolic abnormalities and affective dysregulation in bipolar patients.

Source: Psychiatry Res. 2013;213(3):230–4.
