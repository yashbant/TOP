Newer insights in schizophrenia management: Switching to amisulpride
by Medeka Health | November 01, 2013
Clinical benefits of switching to amisulpride
The efficacy and safety of switching to amisulpride from another antipsychotic has been discussed in several clinical studies. Few such evidences are narrated below.
  • Switch to amisulpride reduces hospitalization rates
  • Switching to amisulpride is effective in the treatmentrefractory schizophrenia
  • Switching to amisulpride improves body weight and metabolic outcomes in overweight or obese schizophrenics
  • Switching to amisulpride improves patient compliance
    • An observational study analyzed the pattern of drug utilization among 570 schizophrenic patients who had been treated with conventional antipsychotics and then switched to amisulpride after 3 months. A total of 50.4% of patients were considered to be compliant under premedication with conventional neuroleptics as well as after being switched to amisulpride (always compliant: 50.4%). While 7.7% patients were always noncompliant, 5.2% were originally compliant and became noncompliant after shifting to amisulpride (worsened compliance), and 36.7% of patients who were noncompliant previously became compliant after switching to amisulpride (responders) ( Figure 2).5


    Patients compliant to amisulpride also showed improved clinical outcomes (PANSS, CGI, Psychosocial Functioning Level [PSFL] and SOFA scores). Hence, switching noncompliant patients from conventional to atypical antipsychotics like amisulpride can improve compliance.5
