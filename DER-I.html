Divalproex extended release is effective and safe in the treatment of acute bipolar mania
by Medeka Health | November 01, 2013
Bowden et al. study

A prospective, randomized, placebo-controlled trial assessed the efficacy and safety of divalproex sodium extended release in hospitalized patients with acute mania associated with bipolar disorder. At baseline, all patients had a Mania Rating Scale (MRS) score of at least 18, with at least 4 items scoring >1. Patients were randomly assigned to divalproex extended release (n=187) or placebo (n=177) for 21 days. Divalproex extended release was started at a dose of 25 mg/kg body weight and increased by 500 mg on day 3; additional dose adjustments were advised only if needed. Regular blood samples were collected to measure the serum concentrations of the drug (target level: 85–125 μg/ mL). By day 5, mean serum valproate level was 95 μg/mL. After 21 days, the drug dose was tapered gradually over 7 days and then stopped. Efficacy of divalproex extended release was measured using the Schedule for Affective Disorders and Schizophrenia-measuring change in symptomology (SADS-C which includes MRS and Depressive Syndrome Scale [DRS] assessed on days 1, 5, 10, 15, and 21) and the Global Assessment Scale (GAS assessed on days 1 and 21). Salient observations of the study include:1

  • Patients treated with divalproex extended release reported a greater improvement on MRS from baseline when compared to patients on placebo (-11.5 vs. -9.0; p=0.013;). Five of the 11 items in MRS showed significant improvements with divalproex extended release when compared to placebo. The 5 items that showed improvement were–need for sleep (p≤0.01), more energy (p≤0.05), increased activity (p≤0.05), generalized motor hyperactivity (p≤0.05), and racing thoughts (p≤0.001).
  • Improvements in Behavior of Ideation Scale (BIS) and Manic syndrome Scale (MSS) scores were higher for patients in the divalproex extended release group than patients in the placebo group (Table 1).
