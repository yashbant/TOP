Amisulpride: Preferred antipsychotic for the treatment of positive symptoms of schizophrenia
by Medeka Health | November 01, 2013
Amisulpride is effective in the treatment of positive symptoms of schizophrenia

Another meta-analysis of 7 randomized trials involving amisulpride showed that the antipsychotic effect of the drug on positive symptoms occurs within the first few weeks of treatment. The study included 1,708 patients with positive symptoms and with at least 2 Brief Psychiatric Rating Scale (BPRS) psychosis items (conceptual disorganization, suspiciousness, hallucinatory behavior, unusual thought content) rated moderate or higher and a total score of at least 12 on the combined 4 psychosis items. The majority of patients were treated with amisulpride (n=1,042), while others were on haloperidol (n=367), flupentixol (n=47), or risperidone (n=252). At baseline, the mean BPRS and psychotic subscores were 58.6±14.5 and 18.1±3.2 respectively. The primary endpoint assessed was the weekly percentage reduction over time (at weeks 1, 2, 4, 13, 26, and 51) in BPRS total score and the psychotic item subscore. Both per protocol (called as observed cases [OC]) and intent-to-treat population (called as last observation- carried-forward analysis [LOCF]) analyses were carried out.6

  • Amisulpride therapy improved BPRS total score and the BPRS psychotic symptom subscore over time in both OC and LOCF analysis (p<0.0001; Figure 1).

  • Pooled data of only two 1-year studies (n=784) showed that significant time effect was retained with amisulpride in reducing both global and psychotic symptoms (p<0.0001; Figure 2)

    .
  • Absolute mean reduction of the BPRS total score at 1 year was 23.1±12.6 points in OC, indicating a reduction of 68% (15.6±11.6) within the first 4 weeks. Similarly, the reduced BPRS score at 1 year for LOCF was 16.2±5.8, with 88% reduction seen within the first 4 weeks (14.2±12.4; Figure 3)

    .

Results of the meta-analysis proved the concept of early onset of antipsychotic action hypothesis. Reductions in global and positive psychotic symptoms with amisulpride were significantly seen within the first and second weeks and were maintained throughout the 1 year of the study duration.
