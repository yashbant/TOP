Escitalopram prevents MSIMI in patients with stable CHD
by Medeka | November 01, 2013
Conclusion
Six weeks of escitalopram therapy is effective in reducing the occurrence of MSIMI in patients with stable CHD. Escitalopram improves serotonergic activity due to its higher selectivity for serotonin reuptake inhibition. Further, escitalopram improves hemodynamic responses and psychological outcomes. Hence, escitalopram is considered to be effective in the treatment of MSIMI.
